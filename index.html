My Story

Language, Literature & Code

I am Hamza El Merdi, born and raised in Youssoufia, Morocco — a city that shaped my sense of community, language, and identity. I pursued my higher education at Université Cadi Ayyad in Marrakech, where I earned a Licence in English Studies with a specialization in Linguistics.

My academic journey gave me a deep understanding of language as a system — from the smallest phonological units to the broad patterns of discourse and pragmatics. I am particularly fascinated by how languages encode meaning, culture, and power.

Beyond linguistics, I have developed a genuine passion for web development and programming. I believe combining language expertise with digital skills opens new frontiers — in natural language processing, educational technology, and cross-cultural digital communication.

"The limits of my language mean the limits of my world."
— Ludwig Wittgenstein · Tractatus Logico-Philosophicus
📖Linguistic Expertise

Deep study of phonology, morphology, syntax, semantics, and pragmatics. Language understood not just as communication but as a cognitive and cultural system.

🌍Translation & Mediation

Trained in translation theory and practice — bridging Arabic, French, and English while preserving meaning, register, and cultural nuance.

✍️Literary Studies

Critical engagement with English and world literature — from Renaissance drama to postcolonial fiction. Texts as mirrors of society and history.

💻Technology & Language

Self-taught in HTML, CSS, JavaScript. Interested in NLP, corpus linguistics tools, and building digital platforms for language learners and researchers.
